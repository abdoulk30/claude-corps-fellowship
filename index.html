01 / cover letter

Background & Why

I spend my weekdays as a paraprofessional for a special needs student in Brooklyn, where I adapt lesson plans, run de-escalation strategies, and sit in meetings with parents and teachers discussing what's best for the student. I spend my nights and weekends building and shipping full-stack products with Claude and other LLMs, then explaining what I built, why I built it, and why someone should use it. Claude Corps is the first opportunity I've come across that puts both of those strengths to use.

I have a B.S. in Computer Science and completed Hack Reactor's full-stack software engineering bootcamp, but what's shaping me most right now is Pursuit's AI-native program. It runs on short build cycles (sometimes a week, sometimes two) where I ship something real and then present it: sometimes with slides, sometimes through narrative storytelling, sometimes just by explaining what I built, its purpose, and why I made the choices I did. Through Pursuit, I've built with Claude and other LLMs, and practiced explaining the product I built and why it would benefit people or a specific company.

What pulls me toward this program is straightforward: it's a real chance to start and grow in the field I actually want to be in, and I'm genuinely interested in getting better at this, not just adding a title. I also try to go after roles I think I'd actually be good at, and this looks like a natural next step from what I'm already doing at Pursuit: building with AI and explaining the result to the people who'll use it, just with more ownership, a longer timeline, and an explicit teaching and handoff component I'm excited to grow into. I want to spend a year doing that at a scale that outlasts me.

06 / diligence statement

How AI was actually used on this site

Following the Creation / Transparency / Deployment diligence framework taught in Anthropic's AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations course.

diligence_statement.md

This site was built with Claude (Anthropic), used through Claude Code. I already knew about Claude before this, but this application is what actually got me using it, and it's since become my favorite LLM. I used it specifically for VentureFlow, one of the projects featured on this page. My other projects, Librio and JobSync, were built using Google Gemini and ChatGPT.

What Claude did: the code. The HTML, CSS, and JavaScript on this page (structure, styling, interactivity) were built in collaboration with Claude.

What I did: the words. Every claim about my background, my projects, and why I want this fellowship is mine: written, reviewed, and something I'd say out loud, because I did.

No confidential or sensitive data went into this build, just my own resume, certificates, and reflections, all information I'm choosing to make public here.

Review process: I read every sentence on this site out loud before it went live and checked it against what actually happened. If a claim didn't sound like me or couldn't be backed up, I rewrote or cut it. The technical project descriptions were checked against the real repos.

Responsibility: I'm the one accountable for everything on this page, technical claims included. If something here is wrong, that's on me, not Claude.

I collaborated with Claude to build this website as part of my application: a way to put real work behind my interest in this opportunity, and to practice the same AI fluency this program is built to evaluate, not just describe it.
